Capability Risk

Two lenses on capability exposure. Scarce vs Stretched evaluates each capability's coverage against the utilization of its holders; Key-Person Risk surfaces capabilities concentrated in one or two individuals.

ⓘ How to read this page

The Scarce vs Stretched view assesses each capability across two dimensions — coverage (the number of practitioners who hold the skill) and utilization (how heavily those practitioners are deployed) — and classifies it into one of four exposure categories:

  • Scarce & stretched — held by few practitioners who are already heavily utilized. The most acute exposure: limited coverage with no spare capacity. Prioritize for hiring or capability transfer.
  • In demand — adequate coverage, but every holder is heavily utilized. The constraint is capacity rather than headcount; rebalance engagements or expand the pool ahead of demand.
  • Niche / watch — held by few practitioners who currently have available capacity. Not an immediate concern, but a single departure would create a gap — a candidate for deliberate succession or cross-skilling.
  • Healthy depth — sufficient coverage with available capacity. No action required.

Thresholds for scarce (number of practitioners) and stretched (utilization) are adjustable via the sliders to reflect the relevant population. The Key-Person Risk lens offers a complementary perspective — capabilities concentrated in one or two individuals, independent of utilization; a ★ denotes a holder at Manager grade or above.
